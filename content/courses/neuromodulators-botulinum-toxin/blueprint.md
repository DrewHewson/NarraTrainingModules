## 1. Positioning

The definitive *introductory* neuromodulator course for nurses entering medical aesthetics: takes a clinically experienced RN/RPN who is **completely new to cosmetic medicine** and builds, from first principles, the knowledge and clinical judgment to safely assess, plan, and — under appropriate supervised practical training — perform **upper-face** neuromodulator treatments, recognize and manage complications, and *think like an experienced injector.*

**Why it beats commercial injector courses:** competency-based and objective-driven (not a lecture dump); anatomy taught *functionally* (muscle → movement → line → rationale); judgment-first assessment (cases, reasoning, algorithms); safety woven throughout; evidence-based with **verified** citations; plain language without losing depth.

## 2. Locked decisions (Andrew, 2026-07-05)

- **Regulatory anchor:** explicit **CNO / Ontario** — controlled acts under the RHPA, orders/medical directives, prescriber vs. administer, Health Canada–approved products.
- **Treatment scope:** **upper face in depth** (frontalis, glabellar complex, lateral canthal lines). Lower face / masseter / neck are **named only** and deferred to a later module.
- **Depth:** **9 chapters, ~10–12 hrs** guided learning (excludes in-person practical).
- **Review cadence:** Andrew gates each chapter; Margie (NP) clinical sign-off on batches.
- **Scope & Safe-Use:** doses/dilutions are *cited educational reference*, technique is taught *conceptually*, supervised in-person practice is the required next step; escalation emphasized in safety chapters.

## 3. Learner & prerequisites

RN/RPN, clinically experienced, new to aesthetics. **Assumed:** general nursing pharmacology, asepsis, consent basics, emergency fundamentals. **Not assumed:** cosmetic facial anatomy, facial assessment, injection planning, cosmetic pharmacology, product selection, dosing, aesthetic complications.

## 4. Module terminal outcomes

On completion the learner can:
1. Explain BoNT-A mechanism at the NMJ and translate it into clinical timelines (onset/peak/duration, reversibility).
2. Compare Health Canada–approved products, explain why units are **not** interchangeable, and describe storage/reconstitution/dilution principles.
3. Analyze upper-face functional anatomy as elevator/depressor pairs and predict the effect of chemodenervation on each muscle.
4. Conduct a structured, client-centred consultation and facial assessment; distinguish dynamic vs. static rhytids; screen unsuitable candidates (incl. BDD).
5. Apply absolute/relative contraindications and significant interactions to a scenario, and obtain valid, well-documented informed consent with standardized photography.
6. Reason about injection depth, dose, diffusion, spacing, and danger zones for the three foundational upper-face areas (conceptually; hands-on is supervised).
7. Build a patient-specific plan documented to a medico-legal standard, and manage aftercare, the 2-week review, and touch-ups.
8. Recognize, prevent, and manage complications using structured algorithms, with clear **escalation/referral** thresholds.
9. Situate all of the above correctly within **CNO scope** and professional ethics.

## 5. Pedagogical rhythm (every chapter)

Learning Objectives → Core Teaching → Clinical Examples → Clinical Pearls → Common Beginner Mistakes → Evidence-Based Best Practice → Recommended Illustrations → Summary → Knowledge Check → References (APA-7, verified). Bloom rises across the module: Understand (Ch1–3) → Apply/Analyze (Ch4–7) → Evaluate/Create (Ch8–9).

**Assessment standard (Andrew, 2026-07-05):** knowledge checks favor **clinical-reasoning/interpretation** items; **True/False is used sparingly** module-wide (prefer scenario MCQ, matching, sequencing). Per-chapter item counts below reflect this — any legacy "T/F" allocation is converted to reasoning unless a crisp factual check is genuinely best.

### Chapter detail

**Ch1 · Orientation — What This Is, Where You Fit & How You'll Think** *(~50m)* — *salvaged & re-anchored from the earlier drafted Section 1.*
LOs: define a neuromodulator (Understand); tell the poison→therapeutic→cosmetic story (Understand); orient to serotypes and which are used in humans (Understand); **situate the RN/RPN in Ontario aesthetic practice — controlled acts under the RHPA, orders/directives, who prescribes vs. administers, professional & ethical obligations** (Understand); navigate how the module works.
Sub: what a neuromodulator is • from foodborne poison to cosmetic milestone • serotype orientation • CNO scope, medical directives & the ethics of aesthetic medicine • how this module works.
Visuals: therapeutic→cosmetic milestone timeline; serotype orientation table; **"Am I authorized to perform this?" CNO decision tree**.
KC: 4 items — 2 MCQ + 2 clinical-reasoning MCQ (reversibility/ptosis; units-not-interchangeable). *No T/F.*

**Ch2 · The Science: Mechanism of Action & Pharmacology** *(~60m)*
LOs: explain ACh signaling at the NMJ (Understand); describe SNAP-25 cleavage blocking release (Understand); translate mechanism into onset/peak/duration & reversibility (Apply).
Sub: NMJ & SNARE complex • toxin structure (heavy/light chain) • four-step intoxication (binding, internalization, translocation, cleavage) • why temporary (axonal sprouting/recovery) • pharmacodynamics (onset ~2–3d, peak ~2wk, duration ~3–4mo) • units as product-specific biological activity.
Visuals: labeled NMJ + SNARE diagram; intoxication-cascade flowchart; onset/peak/duration timeline.
KC: 3 MCQ, 1 matching (component→function), 1 clinical reasoning (atypically short duration).

**Ch3 · The Products: Toxins, Units, Storage & Reconstitution** *(~55m)*
LOs: compare Health Canada–approved BoNT-A products (Understand); explain why units are **not** interchangeable (Analyze); state storage/handling and reconstitution/dilution principles + dose→volume reasoning (Apply).
Sub: ona-/abo-/inco-/praba- (incl. Nuceiva) / daxibotulinumtoxinA • complexing proteins & unit basis • why cross-product conversion is imprecise • storage/cold chain/shelf life • aseptic reconstitution, diluent & volume, concentration↔diffusion trade-off, worked dose→volume examples • labeling/single-patient handling.
Visuals: product comparison table; dilution→concentration reference (with safety caption); reconstitution checklist.
KC: 3 MCQ, 2 matching (product→property; product→unit basis), 1 clinical reasoning (dilution/dose calc).

**Ch4 · Functional Facial Anatomy & the Aging Upper Face** *(~75m)*
LOs: describe layered facial anatomy/SMAS (Understand); predict movement→rhytid per muscle (Apply); identify elevator/depressor antagonist pairs (Analyze); differentiate dynamic vs. static rhytids and the limits of neuromodulators (Analyze).
Sub: facial layers & SMAS • frontalis • glabellar complex (corrugators, procerus, depressor supercilii) • orbicularis oculi • elevator/depressor brow balance • danger-zone thinking • intrinsic vs. extrinsic aging; dynamic→static progression; where toxin helps and where it doesn't.
Visuals: layered anatomy; upper-face muscle map (origin/insertion/action); elevators-vs-depressors periorbital diagram; dynamic-vs-static comparison.
KC: 3 MCQ, 2 matching (muscle→action; muscle→rhytid), 1 clinical reasoning.

**Ch5 · The Upper Face in Depth: Frontalis, Glabella & Lateral Canthal Lines** *(~85m)* · **Practical Competency Checklist anchor (simulated)**
LOs: for each area — targets, landmarks, injection-point logic, typical **cited** dose ranges, area-specific pitfalls (Apply/Analyze); apply depth/dose/diffusion/danger-zone principles (Apply).
Sub: injection principles (depth by plane, dose concepts, diffusion control, spacing, danger zones, asepsis/bruising) • **glabellar complex** (depth logic, ptosis avoidance) • **frontalis** (brow elevation vs. heaviness; treating holistically) • **lateral canthal lines** (superficial, staying lateral, avoiding zygomaticus/smile asymmetry) • elevator/depressor balance in practice.
Each area uses the reasoning template: **Why / How the expert thinks / When / When NOT / What goes wrong / How it's managed.**
Visuals: per-area principle-level illustrations (not step-by-step maps); depth-by-area diagram; per-area common-errors table; upper-face danger-zone map.
Skills: **Competency Checklist** (simulated; for later in-person supervised sign-off).
KC: 3 MCQ, 1 matching (area→landmarks), 1 clinical reasoning.

**Ch6 · The Aesthetic Consultation & Facial Assessment** *(~70m)* · **Case Study 1 (selection) + Case Study 2 (assessment)**
LOs: structure a client-centred consultation eliciting goals/expectations (Apply); screen for BDD/unrealistic expectations (Analyze); perform systematic static & dynamic facial assessment and translate findings to targets (Analyze).
Sub: consultation framework, open questions, real motivation • BDD & red flags, the "no" conversation • facial thirds & symmetry • at-rest vs. dynamic analysis, brow/lid position, documenting baseline asymmetry • rhytid grading concepts • standardized photography (lighting/angles/expression sets/privacy).
Visuals: consultation flowchart; BDD red-flag checklist; facial-thirds diagram; assessment→target-muscle mapping; photography setup & shot list.
KC: 2 MCQ, 1 clinical reasoning; **Case Study 1** (ambiguous candidate → treat/defer/decline), **Case Study 2** (pre-existing brow asymmetry → documentation & targets).

**Ch7 · Contraindications, Interactions & Informed Consent** *(~55m)*
LOs: classify absolute vs. relative contraindications (Analyze); identify significant interactions (Analyze); make a defensible treat/defer/decline decision and obtain/document valid consent (Evaluate/Apply).
Sub: focused aesthetic history (neuromuscular disorders, pregnancy/lactation, aminoglycosides & other agents, allergies/prior product reactions, bleeding risk, infection) • absolute vs. relative contraindications • treat/defer/decline framework • elements of valid consent (capacity, disclosure, voluntariness, specific risks, no-guarantee framing) • medico-legal documentation.
Visuals: absolute-vs-relative contraindication table; drug-interaction table; treat/defer/decline decision tree; consent-elements checklist.
KC: 3 MCQ, 1 matching (condition→absolute/relative/none), 2 clinical reasoning.

**Ch8 · Complications: Prevention, Recognition & Management** *(~65m)* · **Case Study 4 (complication)**
LOs: classify complications by mechanism/severity (Analyze); apply prevention (Apply); execute management + **escalation** algorithms (Evaluate).
Sub: injection-related vs. product-effect; expected vs. adverse • bruising/swelling/pain/headache • **blepharoptosis vs. brow ptosis** (mechanism, timeline, management, apraclonidine as a cited option) • asymmetry & "Spock" brow • diffusion effects • secondary non-response/neutralizing antibodies • red-flag systemic symptoms & when to escalate/refer (e.g., diplopia → urgent ophthalmology) • "dose conservatively + 2-week review" philosophy.
Visuals: complications table (presentation/timeline/prevention/management); ptosis management algorithm; **red-flag/escalation decision tree**.
KC: 3 MCQ, 2 matching (complication→management), 3 clinical reasoning; **Case Study 4** (post-treatment lid heaviness → differentiate & act).

**Ch9 · Planning, Aftercare, Follow-up & Clinical Integration (Capstone)** *(~75m)* · **Case Study 3 (planning) + Case Study 5 (capstone) + OSCE-style station**
LOs: synthesize assessment into a documented, patient-specific plan (Create); tailor dosing to muscle mass/sex/goals ("natural" vs. "frozen") (Evaluate); deliver evidence-based aftercare and structure the 2-week review/touch-up decision (Apply/Evaluate); integrate everything across realistic scenarios and know the edges of competence (Create/Evaluate).
Sub: assessment→plan with rationale • dosing strategy & full-face thinking; when to under-treat and review • aftercare (evidence vs. myth) & rationale • treatment-record documentation (product, lot, units/point, injection map) • 2-week review & touch-up vs. over-treatment • how experts reason (pre-mortems, the pre-injection safety pause, when to refer) • **bridge to supervised practical training.**
Visuals: treatment-planning worksheet; dosing-modifier table; patient-journey flowchart; aftercare sheet (evidence-graded); treatment-record template with injection map; follow-up/touch-up decision tree.
Assessments: **Case Study 3** (first-timer wanting "natural" → full plan), **Case Study 5** (comprehensive capstone), **OSCE-style station** (simulated consult→assessment→plan→consent→safety pause, rubric-scored — judgment & communication, not needle skill).
KC: 2 clinical reasoning.

## 11. Evidence & citation policy

Per the master prompt: graduate-level, **APA-7**, in-text + per-chapter reference list + DOI where available. Source hierarchy: clinical practice guidelines & expert consensus (e.g., published upper-face BoNT-A consensus) → systematic reviews/RCTs → manufacturer product monographs (product-specific units/storage/indications) → major texts; **CNO practice standards & Ontario RHPA/statute for all scope claims.** **No citation, author, journal, or DOI invented.** Any claim whose source isn't verified at drafting is flagged inline (`⚠ verify — Margie sign-off`) rather than asserted. Dosing taught as *ranges with rationale*, deferring to current product monographs and local scope/regulation.
